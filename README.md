📊 Marketing Customer Segmentation Project

End-to-end Data Analytics project leveraging Python (ETL & Data Preprocessing) and SQL (Analytical Modeling & Segmentation) to derive actionable customer insights from a marketing campaign dataset.

🔹 Objective

To analyze customer-level data and build meaningful customer segments using data transformation, feature engineering, and SQL-based analytics, enabling data-driven marketing decisions.

Dataset sourced from Kaggle Marketing Campaign dataset.

🔹 Sub-Objectives
1. Data Cleaning & Preprocessing (Python – ETL Layer)

Performed ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) operations to convert raw, unstructured data into analysis-ready format:

Extract: Loaded raw dataset with inconsistent formatting (tab-separated issues)

Transform (Data Preprocessing & Cleaning): Cleaned and standardized column headers,handled hidden tab characters and structural inconsistencies, removed null and duplicate records, converted data types (numeric, categorical)

Load: Exported clean dataset into structured CSV for downstream analysis

👉 Focus: Data Transformation, Data Quality Improvement, Preprocessing Pipeline

2. Data Analysis & Feature Engineering (SQL Layer)

Performed advanced SQL-based analytics and feature engineering to create business-relevant metrics:

🔸 Feature Engineering:
Age → Derived from Year of Birth

Total Spending → Aggregated across all product categories

Total Purchases → Combined purchase channels

Customer Tenure → Derived from enrollment date

🔸 Customer Segmentation:
Income-based segmentation (Low / Mid / High)

Recency-based segmentation (Active / Warm / Inactive)

Behavioral segmentation using purchase patterns\

🔸 Advanced Analytics:
Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) modeling

Campaign Conversion Rate Analysis

Channel Performance Analysis (Web vs Store vs Catalog)

👉 Focus: SQL Analytics, Aggregations, CASE-based segmentation, Business KPI modeling

🔹 Key Insights:

High-Income Customer Dominance
Customers in higher income brackets contribute disproportionately to total revenue, highlighting the importance of targeted premium marketing strategies.

Channel Preference – Digital First Behavior
Web purchases significantly outperform catalog and store channels, indicating a strong shift toward digital engagement and e-commerce behavior.

Recency Drives Engagement
Customers with recent interactions (<30 days) show higher purchasing activity, validating the importance of recency-based targeting in CRM strategies.

CLV-Based Segmentation Enables Prioritization
By combining Total Spending and Customer Tenure, high-value customers were identified, enabling businesses to:
1.Prioritize retention strategies
2.Optimize marketing spend allocation
3.Design personalized engagement campaigns
